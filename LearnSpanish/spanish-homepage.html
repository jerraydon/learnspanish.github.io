30 Spanish Words and Phrases You Need To Know To Be Fluent

...or at least know enough to get by!

Whether you're planning a trip to Puerto Rico or just wanting to learn to hang with your Spanish-speaking amigos, you're going to want to know these 50 Spanish words and phrases to up your Spanish game. Some may be useful in a pinch: like when you really need to go to the bano or forgot to bring enough dinero for the trip. Others will be good to know for passing conversations with your friends.

Important Spanish Verbs

  1. Estar - to be

    Without getting too deep into Spanish verb conjugation, when you need to describe or even ask where you are or your current mood, you can use the verb estar. Here are a few examples:

    • ¿Dónde estoy? - Where am I?
    • ¿Dónde estás? - Where are you?
    • Estoy aquí. - I'm here.
    • ¿Cómo estás? - How are you?
    • Estoy bien/mal. - I am fine/not well.

  2. Necesitar - to need

    Another important verb to know when you are missing or need something is necesitar. Here are a few examples:

    • Necesito el baño. - I need the bathroom.
    • Lo necesito. - I need it.
    • ¿Necesitas esto? - Do you need this?

  3. Poder - to be able to

    Poder is a verb used to express Whether or not you can do something. Here are some examples:

    • Puedo. - I can.
    • ¿Me puedes ayudar? - Can you help me?
    • ¿Puedo tener esto? - Can I have this?

  4. Tener - to have

    Tener is pretty important to know due to it's many use cases. You use this verb to express whether someone is in possesion of something. Here are some examples:

    • Puedo. - I can.
    • ¿Me puedes ayudar? - Can you help me?
    • ¿Puedo tener esto? - Can I have this?

Spanish You'll Never Want to Forget

  1. ¿Qué dijiste? - What did you say?

  2. ¿Puedes hablar más despacio, por favor?

    Let's face it: Spanish-speakers speak fast. This sentence will help you in that exact situation. It means, "Can you speak slower, please?"

  3. ¿Cómo se dice ... ?

    This will question will be extrememly important for you to learn as it can help you learn Spanish in a pinch. "¿Cómo se dice ... ?" means, "How do you say ... ?" Try pairing it with words like, esto (this) and en español (in Spanish).

  4. ¿Dónde está el baño?

    There is not a worse feeling in the world compared to really needing the bathroom. When you need to ask, "Where is the bathroom?" use this phrase.

  5. Lo siento, no hablo español.

    If all else fails and your faulty Spanish falls on confused ears, just whip out this final phrase. It literally means "I'm sorry, I don't speak Spanish." The Spanish-speaker you're conversing with will completely understand! Learning a new language is hard, so don't sweat it if you have to lean on this. Try adding, "¿Hables inglés?" which means, "Do you speak English?"
